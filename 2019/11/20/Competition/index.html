IROS Service Robot Track

Intorduction

 Ten example tasks, and competition rules are provided with detail.>details< Each team has 60 minutes to gain as many points as possible. All of the ten tasks in the competition will be released two days before the competition day. The competition setups will be accessible one day before the competition.

Our Methods

 In this competition, I am mainly responsible for the positioning of desktop objects. Since I didn’t know the scene environment of the competition, I adopted the traditional method and the deep learning method to test the effect.

 For the traditional method, I mainly use the combination of geometric features, color features and other features of the object to locate. We set the phase frame directly above the desktop (although this may not be the best choice in later competitions). For transparent objects such as ice bucket and water bottle, we infer the center coordinate of the object by extracting the position of non-transparent objects such as water bottle cap and combining with the geometric relationship. For other small objects such as straws placed in the tray, we determine the position information by recognizing the positioning of the tray.

 For the method of deep learning, we adopt the method of instance segmentation. We will provide the object to make data sets for training. Since the factors such as the scene light and the desktop had a great impact on the result, the test effect was not ideal. Before the game, we decided to give up this method and adopt the traditional visual method.

Results

 The teams participating in this competition are universities from around the world such as Cambridge University, Tsinghua University, etc. Finally we won the fourth place in the competition.
